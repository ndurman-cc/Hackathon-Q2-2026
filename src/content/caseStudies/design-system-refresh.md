## Context

The product had grown through several acquisitions. Teams shipped similar patterns with different naming, spacing, and interaction details. Designers and engineers spent time reconciling specs instead of solving user problems.

## Problem

We needed a single source of truth for UI foundations and components that worked for marketing pages and the logged-in app—without blocking squads on a long rewrite.

## Process

- Audited production screens and Figma libraries; clustered duplicates and gaps.
- Ran lightweight interviews with design and engineering leads to prioritize tokens vs. components.
- Built a phased rollout: tokens and typography first, then high-traffic components, with codemods where possible.
- Paired with engineering on accessibility checks (focus, contrast, reduced motion) before each release.

## Outcome

- Documented token set adopted across **three** product surfaces in the first quarter.
- Reduced design QA cycles on new flows by giving reviewers a clear “uses system” checklist.
- Established monthly office hours for contributions and deprecations.

## Reflection

The hardest part was social, not technical: saying no to one-off exceptions. Clear governance and visible leadership support made the difference.

## Context

New customers completed signup but abandoned before finishing setup. Support heard confusion about what to do first and how long the process would take.

## Problem

The onboarding path mirrored internal database steps, not user goals. Progress felt opaque, and mobile breakpoints were an afterthought.

## Process

- Analyzed funnel data and session replays for the first seven days after signup.
- Conducted **eight** contextual interviews with recently onboarded admins.
- Prototyped a job-based flow in Figma; tested with five participants using realistic data tasks.
- Shipped incrementally behind a feature flag; monitored task completion and support volume weekly.

## Outcome

- Median time-to-first-success dropped compared to the prior baseline (replace with your metrics).
- Support tickets tagged “setup” decreased after the second release.
- Marketing and product shared a single narrative for what “done” looks like for new accounts.

## Reflection

Keeping the scope small enough to ship while still addressing root causes required ruthless prioritization. The best ideas we cut became a clear roadmap for the next quarter.
